- 2026-05-22 — **Decision affirmed — the audit registry is an intentional commons; reads stay free by design.** Raised: an agent pays 0.50 USDC via x402 to trigger an audit, then everyone else reads it free — is that a free-rider flaw? Resolved: no — it is the model, and it cannot be changed without breaking the product. Trustless verification (query surface 1 — on-chain `getAudit()` + IPFS-report recompute) *requires* the rootHash and the full report to be public; an audit is public the instant it is anchored. The x402 paywall therefore gates audit **creation**, never **access** — the first agent to need an unaudited contract funds it once, and the resulting audit compounds a shared registry every later agent (and human, and judge) reads for free. The payer is not subsidising strangers: it buys a timely safety decision at the moment that decision has value, and the public artifact it leaves behind grows the commons the whole oracle relies on. Paywalling reads would kill surface 1, the public hallucination-guard count, and the "the engine cannot lie" property — i.e. the entire credibility thesis. **No architecture change.** The real gap was that this intent was implicit; now made explicit on the landing page (`frontend/src/pages/Landing.tsx`): a left-accent "The registry is a commons — by design" callout in the query-surfaces section + a FAQ entry ("If I pay for an audit, why can everyone else read it free?"). The funder-reputation-credit idea (bump the paying agent's ERC-8004 reputation for funding a public audit) was considered and **deferred** — out of scope for the submission window; recorded here as a possible post-hackathon enhancement.
